# PSAC SAS Combined Public Checklist

## Section 1 — Your Plan (PSAC)

_Based on DO-178B/C §11.1, does your Plan for Software Aspects of Certification describe:_
- **(1)** An overview of the system and how software fits into it — functions, architecture, and the hardware/software split?
- **(2)** An overview of the software itself, including each software component and its assigned software level?
- **(3)** The certification basis, and how you determined the software level(s)?
- **(4)** The software life cycle you will follow — the processes from planning through delivery?
- **(5)** Every life-cycle document (data item) you plan to produce, and which ones the FAA will see?
- **(6)** Your schedule for the major software milestones?
## Section 2 — Anything Unusual About Your Project

_Based on DO-178B/C §11.1 (additional considerations), does the PSAC call out:_
- **(1)** Reused or previously developed software, COTS software, or option-selectable software?
- **(2)** Any tools you expect to qualify — and why they need qualification?
- **(3)** Any alternative methods of compliance you plan to propose?
## Section 3 — Suppliers

_Based on DO-178C §11.1 and FAA supplier-oversight expectations:_
- **(1)** If suppliers develop any of your software, does the PSAC explain how you will oversee them?
## Section 4 — Your Results (SAS)

_Based on DO-178B/C §11.20, does your Software Accomplishment Summary:_
- **(1)** Mirror the PSAC's overviews, and state clearly where the project deviated from the plan?
- **(2)** Identify exactly what software was built — part numbers, versions, and key characteristics?
- **(3)** Summarize the software's change history?
- **(4)** State the software's status — including every open problem report and its effect on safety?
- **(5)** Include a compliance statement: your declaration that the software satisfies its objectives?
## Section 5 — Changes After Approval

_Based on DO-178B/C §12.1:_
- **(1)** For modified software, did you analyze the impact of the changes (a change impact analysis) and keep the results?
## Section 6 — The Consistency Check

_Cross-cutting sanity checks before you submit:_
- **(1)** Do the PSAC and SAS agree with each other — same components, same software levels, same processes?
- **(2)** Did you do everything the PSAC promised — and where you didn't, does the SAS explain the deviation?
  - Note: This free checklist is a high-level sanity check — free to use and share. It doesn't include the item-level actions needed to fully close each objective; those live in AeroCert's comprehensive checklist, kept current as the standards change and available through donations. We'd love to hear how it worked for you — tell us at support@aerocert.org.
